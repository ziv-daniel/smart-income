Why 220V Matters for Smart Home in Israel

Understanding Israeli electrical standards is critical before buying any smart home device.

⚡

Israeli Electrical Standard

Israel uses 220V / 50Hz electricity with Type H plugs (the uniquely Israeli 3-pin) and increasingly Type C (European 2-pin). Many smart home products sold globally are designed for the US market (110V / 60Hz) and will not work or may even be dangerous in Israel.

🔥

What Happens with Wrong Voltage

Plugging a 110V-only device into a 220V outlet can cause immediate burnout, fire hazard, or permanent damage. Smart switches installed behind wall plates are especially risky — a fried switch inside your wall is not only expensive but potentially dangerous.

✔

What We Verify

Every product on this page has been checked for 220-240V input range on the device specification sheet. Most quality Zigbee and WiFi devices support 100-240V universal input, but we verify this so you don't have to. Look for the ⚡ Verified in Israel badge.

🔌

Plug Adapters vs. Voltage

A plug adapter changes the physical shape of the plug, not the voltage. If a device supports 220V but has a US-style plug, a simple Type H adapter is all you need. If it only supports 110V, no adapter will help — you need a voltage converter or a different product entirely.

⚠ Important Safety Note

Never install smart switches or relays behind your wall plates without verifying their voltage rating. If you are unsure, consult a licensed electrician (חשמלאי). All in-wall devices on this page support 220V, but always double-check the specific model variant you receive.
