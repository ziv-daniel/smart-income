Understanding Israeli Salary Deductions

Israeli Income Tax Brackets (2024/2025)

Israel uses a progressive income tax system. The more you earn, the higher the rate on the additional income. Here are the current monthly tax brackets:

Monthly Income (NIS) Tax Rate
Up to 7,010 10%
7,010 - 10,060 14%
10,060 - 16,150 20%
16,150 - 22,440 31%
22,440 - 46,690 35%
46,690 - 60,130 47%
Above 60,130 50%

Every Israeli resident receives 2.25 tax credit points (Nekudot Zikuy), each worth approximately 235 NIS per month. Women receive an additional 0.5 points, and parents of young children may receive extra points.

What is Bituach Leumi (National Insurance)?

Bituach Leumi is Israel's national social security system, similar to Social Security in the United States. It provides coverage for:

  • Old-age pensions - Monthly payments after retirement age
  • Disability insurance - Benefits for those unable to work due to disability
  • Unemployment benefits - Financial support during job transitions
  • Maternity leave - Paid leave for new parents
  • Work injury insurance - Coverage for workplace accidents
  • Child allowances - Monthly stipend per child

The employee rate is 0.4% on income up to 60% of the average wage (~7,122 NIS/month) and 7% on income above that threshold, up to the maximum insurable income (~47,465 NIS/month). Health insurance tax is collected alongside Bituach Leumi at rates of 3.1% and 5% respectively.

What is Keren Hishtalmut (Education Fund)?

Keren Hishtalmut is one of Israel's best tax benefits and a unique savings vehicle that does not exist in most other countries. Here's why it's so valuable:

  • Tax-free growth - All returns on the fund are completely tax-free (up to certain limits)
  • Employer match - Your employer contributes 7.5% of your salary while you contribute only 2.5%
  • Short lock-in - The fund matures after just 6 years (3 years if used for education or professional development)
  • No capital gains tax - Upon withdrawal after maturity, you pay zero tax on the gains (up to the ceiling of approximately 15,712 NIS/month in salary)
  • Flexible use - After 6 years, withdraw the entire amount for any purpose

For salaried employees, the employer's 7.5% contribution is essentially free money added to your compensation. Combined with the tax-free growth, Keren Hishtalmut offers returns that are nearly impossible to match with regular investment accounts.

Special Tax Benefits for New Olim (Immigrants)

Israel offers significant tax benefits to new immigrants (olim chadashim) for up to 10 years after making Aliyah:

  • Extra tax credit points - 3 additional credit points for the first 18 months, 2 points for the next 12 months, and 1 point for the following 12 months
  • Foreign income exemption - Complete tax exemption on income earned abroad for 10 years
  • Foreign asset reporting exemption - No requirement to report foreign assets for 10 years
  • Capital gains exemption - Tax exemption on capital gains from foreign assets for 10 years
  • Rental income exemption - Tax-free rental income from foreign properties for 10 years

These benefits make Israel one of the most attractive countries for immigrants from a tax perspective, especially for those with foreign investments, real estate, or remote work income.
